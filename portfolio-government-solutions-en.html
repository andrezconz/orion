S-01 Territorial Analytics

Institutional Effectiveness Diagnostic

"How do we know if the services we provide are really working?"

Rigorous measurement of results of public programs and services using a mixed methodology. We combine quantitative analysis of indicators with qualitative evaluation of processes to offer a comprehensive, actionable diagnostic, ready to present to decision-makers and oversight bodies.

Activities
  • Collection and cleaning of institutional data
  • Descriptive and inferential statistical analysis
  • Interviews with key actors and focus groups
  • Benchmarking against comparable cases
  • Formulation of prioritized recommendations
Deliverables
  • Executive report (max. 30 pages)
  • Interactive dashboard of indicators
  • Presentation for directors
  • Improvement plan with roadmap
Typical Clients
  • District and municipal secretariats
  • Decentralized entities
  • Cooperation agencies
  • Comptrollers and oversight bodies

Duration: 6–12 weeks · Team: 2–3 consultants

S-02 Impact Evaluation

Evaluation of Public Policies and Programs

"How do we show that our policy generated real change and wasn't a coincidence?"

Design and execution of impact evaluations with quasi-experimental methods: differences in differences, regression discontinuity, instrumental variables, and statistical matching. We generate robust evidence on causality that withstands technical and political scrutiny.

Activities
  • Design of the causal identification strategy
  • Construction of panel database
  • Econometric estimation and robustness tests
  • Heterogeneity of effects analysis
  • Validation with academic peers
Deliverables
  • Technical report with detailed methodology
  • Executive summary for decision makers
  • Reproducible code (R / Stata / Python)
  • Public policy note (policy brief)
Typical Clients
  • DNP, Social Prosperity
  • IDB, World Bank, UNDP
  • Sectoral ministries
  • Foundations with social programs

Duration: 8–16 weeks · Team: 2–4 consultants

$40M – $120M COP
S-03 Territorial Intelligence

Territorial Socioeconomic Mapping

"How do we accurately identify where the gaps are and prioritize investment?"

Geospatial analysis of socioeconomic indicators at the sub-local level: multidimensional poverty, access to services, gender gaps, concentration of vulnerabilities. We produce layers of information that allow targeting public investment with defensible technical criteria.

Activities
  • Integration of sources (DANE, SDP, SISBEN)
  • Spatial analysis and index construction
  • Modeling of gaps by territorial unit
  • Cross-referencing with institutional supply data
  • Validation workshops with the community
Deliverables
  • Interactive digital territorial atlas
  • Territorial prioritization index
  • Narrative report with key findings
  • Shapefile and open format data
Typical Clients
  • Planning Secretariats
  • Local mayoralties
  • Local Development Funds
  • USAID, GIZ, EU in Colombia

Duration: 8–14 weeks · Team: 3 consultants + geomático

$30M – $80M COP
S-04 Crisis Management

Institutional Crisis Response Strategy

"How to respond with speed and coordination when everything happens at once?"

Design of protocols, actor mapping, scenario simulations, and contingency plans for entities with responsibility in risk management, citizen security, or social emergencies. We integrate real-time data analysis with inter-institutional coordination frameworks.

Activities
  • Diagnostic of institutional capacities
  • Mapping of risks and critical actors
  • Design of response protocols
  • Simulations and tabletop exercises
  • Crisis communication plan
Deliverables
  • Crisis protocols manual
  • Map of actors and coordination routes
  • Report on institutional vulnerabilities
  • Simulation workshop with management team
Typical Clients
  • District Government Secretariat
  • National Disaster Risk Management Unit
  • Governorships with conflict zones
  • Entities with a public order mandate

Duration: 6–10 weeks · Team: 2 consultants

$15M – $50M COP
