The real challenge

Complexity is not solved with more data, but by understanding who can act.

  • Fragmented information among institutions, territory, and market.
  • Lagging indicators that do not explain what is happening right now.
  • Actors with different incentives, unequal capacities, and conflicting agendas.
  • Policies and strategies that do not connect diagnosis, execution, and measurement.

Methodology

ORION Intelligence Framework

A work system to move from complex data and conflicting actors to evidence-based decisions.

1. Intelligence Discovery Core question: What is the actual decision problem? Defining the challenge, mapping stakeholders, theory of change, problem tree, objectives, and hypotheses without discussing data yet.
2. Data Integration Building the evidence base. Integration of sources (DANE, CEDE, Registraduría, SECOP, open data, GIS, surveys, and social media) via robust ETL processes.
3. Analytical Modeling Analytical modeling. Application of econometrics, machine learning, clustering, forecasting, networks, NLP, and spatial analysis. Output: analytical models, not decisions.
4. Decision Intelligence The core differentiator of ORION. Transforming models into optimal decisions through scenario analysis, risk modeling, prioritization, and actionable recommendations.
5. Policy Design Designing public policies and interventions once the decision is made: defining programs, territorial projects, goals, and key performance indicators (KPIs).
6. AI & Automation Automation and technical productivity. Integrating large language models (LLMs), RAG systems, smart assistants, and automated executive report generation.
7. Monitoring & Learning Feedback and continuous improvement. Quantitative impact evaluation, executive dashboards, and alert systems to continually improve public policy.
